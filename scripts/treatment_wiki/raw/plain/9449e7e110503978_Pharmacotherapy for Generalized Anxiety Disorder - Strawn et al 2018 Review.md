# Pharmacotherapy for Generalized Anxiety Disorder (GAD) - Strawn et al 2018 Review

## Treatment Lines

### First-line (Adults)
SSRIs and SNRIs represent the first-line psychopharmacologic treatment for GAD in adults.

### Second-line (Adults)
Buspirone (azapirone), benzodiazepines, second-generation antipsychotics (SGAs), and pregabalin.

### Third-line (Adults)
Tricyclic antidepressants (TCAs) such as imipramine.

## SSRI Dosing for GAD
- Escitalopram: 10-20 mg/day. Superior to placebo in 3 pooled 8-week trials. Relapse prevention at 24-76 weeks.
- Paroxetine: 20-50 mg/day. FDA-approved for GAD. Higher adverse event rate vs escitalopram. Withdrawal symptoms more common.
- Sertraline: 50 mg/day adult effective dose. Evidence in pediatric GAD (25-50 mg/day ages 6-17).
- Fluoxetine: 20 mg/day pediatric dosing. Ages 7-17. 61% response vs 35% placebo.

## SNRI Dosing for GAD
- Venlafaxine XR: 75-225 mg/day. First FDA-approved antidepressant for GAD. 69% response vs 46% placebo at 6 months. 12 months treatment preferred to decrease relapse risk.
- Duloxetine: 60-120 mg/day (flexibly dosed). FDA-approved for GAD. Meta-analysis RR=1.60 for remission. Relapse prevention: 41.8% duloxetine vs 13.7% placebo. Effective in elderly patients (65+ years). ONLY antidepressant with FDA approval for pediatric GAD (30-120 mg/day ages 7-17). Dose range 60-120 mg daily demonstrated efficacy in most studies.

## Second-Line Pharmacotherapies

### Benzodiazepines
- Most effective class by effect size: Hedges g=0.50 (vs SNRIs 0.36, SSRIs 0.33)
- Alprazolam: only BZD FDA-approved for GAD
- Clonazepam, lorazepam, diazepam: long-acting preferred
- Use: short-term (3-6 months), for low-risk patients when other treatments contraindicated
- Key finding: Alprazolam more effective for somatic symptoms; imipramine more effective for psychic symptoms

### Buspirone
- 5-HT1A receptor agonist, non-benzodiazepine anxiolytic
- Cochrane review: effective, especially in benzodiazepine-naive patients
- First-line augmentation for inadequate antidepressant response with anxiety symptoms (Level 1 evidence)
- Can improve cognitive function in anxiety patients

### Pregabalin
- Dose: 150-600 mg/day (higher doses = more side effects)
- Separation from placebo within ~1 week (rapid onset)
- Faster onset than venlafaxine
- Hedges g=0.364 (psychic: 0.349; somatic: 0.24)
- Effective in older adults and as adjunctive in antidepressant-resistant GAD
- Side effects: dizziness, somnolence, dose-dependent weight gain
- Abuse liability concern: may be more addictive than gabapentin

### Second-Generation Antipsychotics (SGAs) for Augmentation
- Quetiapine XR: 50-300 mg/day. Relapse prevention superior to placebo. Weight gain notable.
- Risperidone: 0.5-1.5 mg/day adjunctive. Greater HAM-A reductions vs placebo (p=0.034).
- Olanzapine: mean 8+/-7 mg/day adjunctive to fluoxetine. Significant weight gain.
- Aripiprazole: Low-dose adjunctive. 2 open-label studies in treatment-resistant GAD. Partial D2/5-HT1A agonist.

## Somatic vs Psychic Symptom Targeting
- Somatic symptoms/hyperarousal respond best to GABAergic medications (benzodiazepines, pregabalin)
- Psychic symptoms (dysphoria, worry) respond best to noradrenergic/serotonergic agents (TCAs, SSRIs, SNRIs)
- Duloxetine dual mechanism (5-HT + NE) may provide advantages for somatic symptoms: cross-condition evidence from MDD+pain, neuropathic pain (84.2% studies support first-line), fibromyalgia (77.8% positive)

## Augmentation Strategies for Treatment-Resistant GAD
1. SSRI/SNRI + SGA (olanzapine, risperidone, quetiapine XR, aripiprazole)
2. SSRI/SNRI + Pregabalin (evaluated in antidepressant-resistant GAD)
3. SSRI/SNRI + Benzodiazepine (more rapid stabilization, circumvents transient SSRI-initiation anxiety increase)
4. SSRI/SNRI + Buspirone (especially for patients with prominent anxiety symptoms, Level 1 evidence)
5. SSRI/SNRI + Eszopiclone (for patients with prominent insomnia)

## Treatment Duration and Principles
- SSRI/SNRI trials should be at least 8 weeks
- If no response by week 8, low likelihood of additional improvement
- Optimal minimum treatment duration: 12 months in adults
- Initiate at low doses, titrate at 1-2 week intervals over 4-6 weeks
- Full response may take up to 12 weeks or more

## Comparative Effect Sizes (Hedges g)
- Benzodiazepines: 0.50
- Pregabalin: 0.364
- SNRIs: 0.36
- SSRIs: 0.33
