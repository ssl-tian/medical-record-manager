# Duloxetine Dosing and Efficacy for Generalized Anxiety Disorder - Systematic Review

Rodrigues-Amorim et al. 2020, Frontiers in Psychiatry. 11 studies, 2,608 patients.

## Dose Range
- Therapeutic range: 60-120 mg/day demonstrated efficacy in most studies
- Approved dose range: 20-120 mg/day
- Starting dose: typically 30-60 mg/day, flexibly titrated upward

## Dose Escalation Evidence
- All 11 GAD studies used flexible-dose designs starting at 30-60 mg/day, up to 120 mg/day
- Flexible-dose duloxetine 60-120 mg showed statistically significant improvement on HAMA in all studies (P <= 0.05)
- Non-inferiority vs venlafaxine for GAD
- Relapse prevention: duloxetine 60-120 mg over 26 weeks significantly reduced relapse vs placebo (P=0.028)
- Elderly patients (mean age 71): duloxetine 30-120 mg showed significant HAMA improvement (P<0.001)
- Pediatric GAD (ages 7-17): duloxetine 30-120 mg/day. Response rate 59% vs 42% placebo. Remission 50% vs 34%.

## Somatic Symptom Evidence
- Dual mechanism (5-HT + NE reuptake inhibition) directly targets somatic symptoms
- Somatic symptoms (muscle tension, pain, fatigue, dizziness) linked to aberrant 5-HT and NE neurotransmission
- Cross-condition evidence: 84.2% of neuropathic pain studies support duloxetine as first-line
- Fibromyalgia: significant BPI score improvements in 77.8% of studies
- HAMA total score captures somatic anxiety subscale (muscular, sensory, cardiovascular, respiratory, GI, autonomic)

## Key Pharmacological Properties
- Half-life: approximately 12 hours
- Metabolized by CYP1A2 and CYP2D6
- Moderate protein binding (>90%)
- Dual reuptake inhibition: 5-HT > NE at lower doses; balanced at higher doses

## Pediatric-Specific
- ONLY antidepressant with FDA approval for pediatric GAD (ages 7-17)
- PARS improvement: duloxetine -9.7 vs placebo -7.1 (p<=0.001, Cohen's d: 0.5)
- Dose-independent hemodynamic effects (systolic BP, heart rate increases)
- Small but significant weight loss observed

Source: Rodrigues-Amorim D et al. A Systematic Review of Efficacy, Safety, and Tolerability of Duloxetine. Front Psychiatry. 2020. PMC7644852.
