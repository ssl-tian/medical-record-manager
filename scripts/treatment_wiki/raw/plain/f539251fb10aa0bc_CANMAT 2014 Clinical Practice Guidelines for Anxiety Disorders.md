# CANMAT 2014 Clinical Practice Guidelines for Anxiety Disorders - Treatment Framework

Canadian clinical practice guidelines for the management of anxiety, posttraumatic stress and obsessive-compulsive disorders. Katzman et al. BMC Psychiatry 2014.

## General Pharmacotherapy Approach

### First-line Agents
SSRIs and SNRIs are usually preferred as initial treatments for most anxiety disorders. They are generally safer and better tolerated than TCAs or MAOIs. Various antidepressants including SSRIs, SNRIs, NaSSAs, TCAs, MAOIs, and RIMAs have demonstrated some efficacy.

### Benzodiazepines
Useful as adjunctive therapy early in treatment, particularly for acute anxiety or while waiting for antidepressant efficacy. Use should usually be restricted to short-term use (2-4 weeks) due to concerns about dependency, sedation, cognitive impairment, and other side effects.

### Atypical Antipsychotics
Because of risks of diabetes and weight gain, and limited RCT evidence in anxiety disorders, atypical antipsychotics (quetiapine, olanzapine, risperidone, aripiprazole, ziprasidone) are generally recommended as second-line, third-line, or adjunctive therapies.

### Anticonvulsants
Several anticonvulsants have demonstrated efficacy in some anxiety disorders (including pregabalin for GAD), but are generally recommended as second-line, third-line, or adjunctive therapies. Risks include rash, blood dyscrasias, and need for monitoring.

## General Principles of Pharmacotherapy
- Initiate medication at low doses and titrate at 1- to 2-week intervals over 4-6 weeks
- Full response may take up to 12 weeks or more
- Continue therapy for at least 12-24 months to prevent relapse
- Follow-up every 1-2 weeks initially, then monthly after 6 weeks
- Monitor for weight changes, metabolic effects, and other adverse events
- Abrupt discontinuation of SSRIs/SNRIs can cause discontinuation syndrome; slow tapering advised

## Comparison of CANMAT vs International Guidelines
- SSRIs/SNRIs are consistently first-line across all major guidelines (CANMAT, BAP, NICE, APA)
- Benzodiazepine recommendations vary: CANMAT restricts to short-term adjunctive use
- Pregabalin: recommended as second-line in CANMAT for GAD specifically
- Augmentation with SGAs: reserved for treatment-resistant cases across all guidelines
